Magnetic Spring

Device Information

The magnetic spring is commonly referred to as a "reed switch". It got this name because when they are placed in a row look like reeds. The device has two pieces of metal very close to each other - a close magnet can pull on the metal and cause them to connect, allowing current to flow. Because these devices are so simply, they are reliable and have a long lifespan. It is the equivalent of a binary hall-effect sensor - either HIGH or LOW output. This device has had an important role in early computing, but usage has decreased as other devices have replaced its functionality.

Applications

  • Magnetic sensor - check if a door is closed
  • Used in some electronic instruments such as organs
