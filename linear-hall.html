Linear Hall Effect Sensor

Device Information

A Hall Effect sensor can measure tha magnitude of a magnetic field next to the device. The Hall effect occurs when current flows into a magnetic field. If the magnetic is oriented correctly, positve and negative charge will be pushed to two different sides of the wire or plate it is moving through. This voltage difference across the conductor is the Hall effect. Hall Effect sensors are both cheap and reliable, and have many uses throughout the engineering occupations.

Applications

  • Buttons - under the button would be a magnet you push closer to the device
  • Measuring pressure measurements - pressure pushes a magnet causing the Hall effect (Biomedical Applications)
  • Ensuring a safety belt is plugged in
  • Fuel Level Detection - magnet attached to a float, varies in height depending on tank fill
